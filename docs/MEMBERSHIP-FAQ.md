# OpenSeat Membership FAQ

General answers for restaurant membership clubs powered by OpenSeat. Use this as a reusable FAQ for website copy, chat responses, onboarding messages, or support handoffs.

---

## What is the membership club?

It is the restaurant’s retention program inside OpenSeat.

Members can:
- collect points and visit progress
- unlock rewards and claims
- receive relevant club updates
- share referral codes when the restaurant enables bring-a-friend flows

The exact rewards and tone depend on the restaurant, but the mechanics stay consistent.

---

## How does someone join?

Most restaurants add members automatically when they book, visit, or explicitly opt into the club.

A good operator answer:

"Once you book or visit with us, we can attach your profile to the club and track your benefits."

---

## How are points earned?

By default, points are earned from completed visits.

What members usually care about:
- points are tied to real completed visits
- rewards become claimable once the guest has enough balance
- the dashboard and member summary show the current balance clearly

If a restaurant chooses to add manual bonuses, those should be explained as one-off perks, not as the default rule.

---

## Are there tiers?

Yes. OpenSeat supports loyalty tiers so regulars and VIP guests can be recognized differently.

Simple explanation:

"The more completed visits you have, the stronger your membership status becomes."

If the restaurant wants to explain exact thresholds publicly, keep that copy consistent everywhere.

---

## What is the stamp card?

The stamp card is a visit-progress view.

In the standard setup:
- each completed visit adds one stamp
- a full card unlocks the configured bonus

This is useful for guests who understand progress faster than raw point balances.

---

## How do rewards work?

A member can claim a reward once they have enough balance.

Operationally:
- the guest claims the reward through the agent or with staff help
- the system generates a claim code
- staff can verify and redeem the claim in the dashboard

Best explanation to guests:

"When you have enough balance, the reward becomes available. Once you claim it, the team can honor it with your claim code."

---

## How does bring-a-friend / referral work?

If the restaurant enables referrals:
- existing members can receive a referral code
- a new guest can be attributed to that member
- the restaurant can reward the referrer, the new guest, or both

Clean guest-facing answer:

"If you have a referral code, share it with a friend. When they join through that code, the club can recognize both sides of the referral."

Do not promise the exact reward unless that restaurant already has a live referral offer configured.

---

## Can members opt out of marketing or club messages?

Yes.

OpenSeat separates:
- essential reservation communication
- optional membership/promotional messaging

So a guest can opt out of club-style messaging while still receiving important reservation updates.

Recommended wording:

"You can turn off membership/promotional messages and still receive essential booking updates."

---

## Why did a member stop receiving club messages?

Usually one of three reasons:
- they opted out
- the restaurant paused or changed the campaign
- there is no relevant live message to send right now

Do not guess. Operators should check the guest profile and loyalty summary before answering.

---

## Can a reward expire or become unavailable?

Yes.

A reward may be unavailable because:
- it is inactive
- the guest no longer has enough balance
- the reward was already claimed/redeemed
- the restaurant changed the current offer set

Good answer:

"Some rewards are only available while the offer is active and your balance still qualifies for it."

---

## What should staff say if a guest asks, "What do I get from joining?"

Keep it short and concrete:

"You collect visit progress, unlock rewards over time, and sometimes get referral or comeback perks when they fit."

Avoid listing every possible perk. One clear promise is better than a long menu.

---

## What should staff say if a guest asks, "How do I use my reward?"

Use this answer:

"Once the reward is claimable, we can issue a code for it and honor it here through the dashboard."

If the guest already has a claim code, staff should verify the claim instead of explaining theory.

---

## What should staff avoid saying?

Avoid:
- promising a reward that is not active
- inventing a referral bonus on the spot
- saying a guest is opted out without checking
- mixing reservation updates with marketing consent

If there is any doubt, check the guest profile or membership summary first.
