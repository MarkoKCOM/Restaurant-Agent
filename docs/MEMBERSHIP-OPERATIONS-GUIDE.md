# Membership Operations Guide

A practical operator guide for running loyalty, rewards, referrals, and messaging in OpenSeat.

This document is intentionally general. It is not tied to one restaurant brand.

---

## 1. What a healthy membership program looks like

A strong club is not a giant discount engine.

It should do four things well:
- reward real repeat behavior
- help staff recognize valuable guests quickly
- make referrals easy to understand
- keep messaging useful enough that guests do not opt out

If the club creates confusion for staff or feels vague to guests, simplify it.

---

## 2. Where operators should work in OpenSeat

### Loyalty dashboard
Use this for:
- member volume and repeat-guest visibility
- referral advocates and referred guests
- referral-ready rewards
- overall program health
- active reward management

### Guest profile
Use this for one specific member:
- current balance and tier
- active claims
- streak and referral status
- messaging preference state
- notes that matter for hospitality

### Reward management
Use this for:
- creating rewards
- activating/deactivating rewards
- choosing reward templates with clear moments
- keeping referral offers live when bring-a-friend is part of the strategy

---

## 3. Launch checklist

Before promoting the club, confirm these basics:
- loyalty is enabled for the restaurant
- there is at least one clear starter reward
- staff know how to verify and redeem reward claims
- referral flow is either clearly enabled or clearly not promoted
- member messaging copy is useful and not spammy
- operators know where to check opt-out status

If referrals are important to the business, also confirm:
- members can get referral codes
- there is at least one live referral-ready reward
- the team knows how to explain the referral offer in one sentence

---

## 4. Reward strategy: keep it legible

A reward should answer three questions immediately:
- what is the guest getting?
- when should we offer it?
- can staff honor it without friction?

Good examples:
- dessert on the next visit
- midweek discount for comeback guests
- starter for the table on bring-a-friend bookings
- birthday table treat

Weak rewards usually fail because they are vague, too conditional, or hard for the floor team to honor.

---

## 5. Referral playbook

Referrals should feel natural, not like coupon spam.

### What to make visible
Operators should always be able to see:
- which guests already have referral codes
- who has successfully referred someone
- which guests were attributed to a referral
- whether there is a live reward that supports bring-a-friend

### Good referral framing
Use simple language:
- "Bring a friend and we’ll take care of dessert"
- "Come as a small group and the table gets a starter on us"

Avoid complicated referral math in guest-facing copy.

### Weekly referral review
Check the loyalty dashboard for:
- top advocates
- referred guest count
- whether referral-ready rewards are active
- whether referred guests are actually returning

If referral activity is flat, the issue is usually one of these:
- no live referral offer
- staff are not mentioning it
- the reward is too weak or too confusing

---

## 6. Messaging preferences and consent

Treat this seriously.

OpenSeat distinguishes between:
- essential reservation communication
- optional loyalty/promotional messaging

Operational rule:
- do not treat a reservation update as permission for club marketing
- do not treat a club opt-out as a reason to stop essential booking communication

When a guest opts out:
- respect it immediately
- confirm internally that the guest profile reflects the change
- do not try to override it with manual marketing outreach through the same workflow

---

## 7. Staff redemption workflow

When a guest says they have a reward:
1. verify the claim code in the dashboard
2. confirm the reward is still active/pending
3. honor it at service
4. redeem it in the dashboard so the record stays clean

Important:
- staff should not improvise around expired or already-redeemed claims
- if something looks wrong, escalate to an operator/admin instead of guessing

---

## 8. Daily operator routine

### Start of shift
- open the loyalty dashboard
- scan top members and referral activity
- check whether any reward claims or VIP notes may affect service today

### During service
- use guest profiles for context when needed
- verify claims instead of handling them informally
- note strong regulars, celebrations, or hospitality moments that matter later

### End of day
- make sure completed visits were marked correctly
- spot-check whether active rewards still match current business goals
- review whether referral and comeback offers are still the right ones to keep live

---

## 9. Weekly operator review

Once a week, review:
- total members
- repeat-guest trend
- opted-out guest count
- active vs inactive rewards
- referral advocates
- referred guests
- whether referral rewards are actually being used

If you see opt-outs rising, fix message quality before increasing volume.

If you see rewards growing but not being claimed, simplify the catalog.

If you see referred guests but no repeat visits, improve the follow-up offer after the first referral visit.

---

## 10. Suggested internal rules

Use these as defaults:
- one clear referral offer is better than three weak ones
- one or two strong reward templates beat a bloated catalog
- staff should be able to explain any live reward in one sentence
- never promise a perk that is not currently active
- always check opt-out state before discussing campaign messaging issues

---

## 11. Recommended handoff lines for staff

### When a guest asks about the club
"You collect visit progress, unlock rewards over time, and sometimes get referral or comeback perks when they fit."

### When a guest asks about referrals
"If your profile has a referral code, share it with a friend and we can recognize that visit properly on both sides."

### When a guest asks to stop club messages
"No problem — we can switch off loyalty/promotional messaging and you’ll still get essential reservation updates."

### When a guest wants to use a reward
"Let me verify the claim code and we’ll take care of it here."
